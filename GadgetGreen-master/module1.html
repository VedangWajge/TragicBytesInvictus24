EFFECTS

ENVIRONMENTAL IMPACT

Toxic Material

E-waste contains hazardous substances like lead, mercury, cadmium, and brominated flame retardants, which can contaminate soil and water when not managed correctly.

Air and Water Pollution

Improper disposal methods, such as open burning or inadequate recycling, release harmful pollutants into the air and water, contributing to pollution.

Resource Depletion

The production of electronic devices consumes significant amounts of finite resources like metals and minerals, leading to habitat destruction and resource depletion.

HEALTH RISK

Neurological Effects

Exposure to lead and other toxins in e-waste can impair cognitive development in children and cause neurological issues in adults.

Respiratory Problems

Inhalation of hazardous fumes during e-waste processing can result in respiratory issues, including lung irritation.

Skin Disorders

Contact with certain e-waste components can lead to skin disorders and allergies.
